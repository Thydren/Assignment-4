Alan Turing

Alan Turing was an English Mathematician who served during World War II and was eventually considered to be the father of modern computing. He was born June 23rd 1912 and died June 7th 1954. He is known for his work in World War II where he was responsible for deciphering and breaking Nazi codes. He also created what is known as the Turing Machine which is a basic, primitive version of a computer. Although he accomplished many amazing things, because he was gay, he was prosecuted by his own country. Alan Turning lead a life of knowledge and learning that is now being recognized.
